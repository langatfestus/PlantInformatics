Did you Know ??

Maize, also known as corn, is a cereal grain that originated in Mesoamerica and
is now cultivated worldwide. It is a versatile crop used for food, animal feed,
and industrial products. Maize is rich in carbohydrates, dietary fiber,
and essential nutrients such as vitamins A and C. It is consumed in various forms,
including fresh, dried, and processed into products like cornmeal, cornflour, and corn syrup.

Wheat is a cereal grain widely grown and consumed globally. It is a staple food for a large
portion of the world's population and is used to make various products such as bread, pasta,
noodles, and pastries. Wheat grains contain carbohydrates, proteins, dietary fiber, and essential
nutrients like B vitamins and minerals. Wheat cultivation dates back to ancient times, and today,
it is cultivated in diverse climates and environments, contributing significantly to human nutrition
and food security worldwide..

Beans are plants that produce seeds we eat. They're like little protein powerhouses and come in
different types, such as kidney beans, black beans, and chickpeas. People cook them and eat
them in dishes like soups, stews, and salads.
